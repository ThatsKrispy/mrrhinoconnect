Get Off Copper Before the Price Hike Forces You

The FCC's 2022 ruling allowing carriers to decommission legacy copper POTS (Plain Old Telephone Service) lines has triggered dramatic price increases across South Florida — AT&T, Lumen, and other carriers are raising POTS rates 200–500% in markets where they're sunset-ing legacy infrastructure. MrRhinoConnect helps commercial and multi-family property owners migrate away from expensive POTS lines before the pain gets worse.

Most commercial buildings still rely on POTS for elevator emergency phones (required by ASME A17.1), fire alarm autodialers (required by NFPA 72), gate operator systems, and fax lines. Each has a cost-effective modern replacement — and we handle all of them.

POTS Replacement Solutions for South Florida Properties

Miami-Dade and Broward County have among the highest concentrations of older commercial buildings in Florida — many built in the 1980s and 1990s when POTS infrastructure was standard for every building system. As carriers sunset copper networks and raise rates, owners of these properties face significant cost increases unless they act proactively.

Life Safety System Compliance

The most critical POTS replacements — elevator emergency phones and fire alarm autodialers — are life safety systems governed by ASME A17.1 and NFPA 72 respectively. Not all VoIP or cellular solutions are certified compliant. MrRhinoConnect specifies and installs only equipment that meets the applicable life safety codes, and we coordinate with your AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction) when required.

Cost Analysis: POTS vs. Alternatives

A single POTS line in South Florida currently costs $60–$200/month and rising. A cellular communicator for the same application costs $15–$35/month with a one-time hardware investment of $200–$600. For a building with 8–10 POTS lines, the annual savings after migration often exceed $10,000 — paying back the migration project in under 12 months.
