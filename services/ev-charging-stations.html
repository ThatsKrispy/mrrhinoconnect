EV Charging Installation Across South Florida's Condos, Hotels & Commercial Parks

Florida law now requires condominium associations to accommodate EV charging requests from unit owners under FS 718.113 and FS 720.337. This has created significant demand for retroactive EV infrastructure in South Florida's aging condo stock — and MrRhinoConnect has the experience to design cost-effective solutions that serve multiple residents without overloading building electrical panels.

Electrical Infrastructure Planning

The biggest cost in EV charging installation is often the electrical service upgrade, not the chargers themselves. Our electrical team assesses your existing service capacity, designs a load management strategy, coordinates with FPL or TECO for necessary upgrades, and installs the panel work — all under permit.

Incentives & Utility Rebates

Florida Power & Light's EV charging rebate programs, the federal Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Tax Credit (30C), and FDOT grant programs can significantly offset installation costs. We provide documentation to support all applicable rebate and incentive applications as part of our service.
